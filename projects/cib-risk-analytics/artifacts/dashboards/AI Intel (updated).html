AGENT MEMORY ARCHITECTURE

Layer 1 — In-Context Working Memory (200K–1M tokens): Current task state, conversation, retrieved documents. Ephemeral per session. With Opus Context Compaction: effectively infinite via automatic summarization. Fast, immediate, expensive at scale.

Layer 2 — External Vector Memory / RAG (Long-Term Institutional Knowledge): Embeddings of proprietary risk policies, stress scenarios, regulatory guidance, model documentation — stored in vector databases (Pinecone, pgvector, Weaviate). Retrieved on-demand via semantic similarity. The institutional knowledge moat. This is where CIB AI competitive advantage lives — proprietary knowledge embedded in RAG, not access to the same public models every competitor uses.

Layer 3 — Structured State Memory (Databases): Traditional relational/time-series databases for workflow state, audit trails, limit data, position data. Deterministic, queryable, auditable. Required for regulatory compliance. The governance layer that makes agents enterprise-deployable.

Governance Imperative — Four Questions Every Agent Workflow Must Answer: (1) What decisions can the agent make autonomously vs. what requires mandatory human checkpoint? (2) What is the complete audit trail — every decision, tool call, data source? (3) How is agent failure detected and what is the recovery path? (4) What is the agent's data access scope — what can it never touch? Without explicit answers to all four, any production agent in a regulated environment is a compliance and reputational risk.

Anthropic's Microscope (2024–2025): Built a sparse autoencoder (second model) trained to transparently mimic Claude's internal behavior. 2024: identified features for recognizable concepts (Michael Jordan, Golden Gate Bridge). 2025: traced complete sequences from prompt to response. Open-sourced attribution graphs — research community can run circuit tracing on Claude, visualize computational graphs, modify feature values and observe output changes. Anthropic goal: "reliably detect most AI model problems by 2027."

Chain-of-Thought Monitoring: Technique for "listening in" on reasoning model inner monologue during extended thinking. OpenAI used this to catch a reasoning model cheating on coding tests. Now standard evaluation technique for advanced reasoning models.

Model Organisms of Misalignment: Controlled demonstrations of AI systems with known misaligned goals — study how alignment failures arise before they appear in production. Most recent: stress-tested 16 frontier models in simulated corporate environments where models could autonomously send emails. Results inform agentic governance frameworks.

Constitutional AI: Training models to self-critique outputs against a principle set — Claude's foundational safety mechanism. Scales without proportionally more human oversight. The enterprise reliability architecture. Anthropic's Claude model specification (constitutionification) is public.

ALIGNMENT FRONTIERRLHF / DPO / RLAIF Evolution · Scalable Oversight · AI Control Research

Post-RLHF landscape: RLHF (expensive human labeling) → DPO (Direct Preference Optimization, no separate reward model, faster, now preferred) → RLAIF (RL from AI feedback, AI generates preference data at scale). Constitutional AI = principled RLAIF variant. Most 2026 frontier models combine all three with RLAIF increasingly dominant.

Scalable oversight problem: As AI becomes more capable than humans at specific tasks, how do humans evaluate whether AI output is correct? This is the fundamental alignment challenge. Current approaches: debate (have two AIs argue, human judges argument quality), amplification (use AI to check AI), interpretability (examine internal computations directly). None fully solved. Anthropic's 2027 interpretability target is the most concrete proposed solution.

AI Control research: What guardrails prevent highly capable AI from taking harmful autonomous actions even if its values are subtly misaligned? Independent of alignment (values) — control means: even a misaligned AI behaves safely because it cannot take unilateral actions that bypass human oversight. Actively researched at Anthropic, ARC, Redwood Research. Spring 2026 SPAR projects include "neural circuit breakers" detecting deception/power-seeking behavior vectors before execution.

The Papers That Built This — Essential Reading

The intellectual foundation of the 2026 AI landscape. Organized by tier: foundational architecture, agentic and memory systems, alignment and safety, and financial AI applications. TIER 1 — FOUNDATIONAL ARCHITECTURE
TIER 1
"Attention Is All You Need" — Vaswani et al., Google Brain, 2017
100,000+ citations // Most cited ML paper of all time
Introduced the Transformer — every frontier LLM's architecture. Self-attention replaces recurrent networks, enables parallel processing and long-range dependency modeling. Understanding attention mechanisms is understanding how LLMs represent and relate concepts in context windows from 200K to 10M tokens.
TIER 1
"Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models" — Kaplan et al., OpenAI, 2020
Mathematical basis for the scaling era // Justified $527B 2026 capex
Power-law relationships between compute, data, parameters, and capability — the mathematical foundation for "more compute = more capable." DeepSeek V4's $0.10/M pricing and MoE efficiency challenge whether brute-force scaling is the only path, but the laws themselves remain foundational to understanding capability development.
TIER 1
"Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback" — Bai et al., Anthropic, 2022
Foundation of Claude's safety architecture // Dominant enterprise AI safety paradigm 2026
Training models to self-critique against a defined principle set (constitution), revising until compliant — without human labeling of every example. Scales to frontier models without proportionally more human oversight. This is why regulated industries prefer Claude: Constitutional AI is the mechanism behind reliability in high-stakes work.
TIER 1
"DeepSeek-R1: Incentivizing Reasoning via RL" — DeepSeek-AI, Jan 2025
The Sputnik moment paper // $6M training vs $100M+ Western equivalents
Reasoning-class performance matching OpenAI o1 at a fraction of cost using GRPO. Triggered January 2025 $1T tech selloff. Proved efficiency-focused architectures close the gap with brute-force scaling. Launched the global efficiency revolution defining 2026's competitive dynamics — and directly caused DeepSeek V4's $0.10/M pricing ambition.
TIER 2 — AGENTIC & MEMORY SYSTEMS
TIER 2
"ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in LLMs" — Yao et al., Princeton/Google, 2022
Foundation of all agent frameworks // The Reasoning+Acting paradigm
Established the ReAct pattern — LLMs interleaving reasoning with action calls (tool use, web search, code execution). Every modern agent framework (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, Claude Code, ADK) descends from this paradigm. Think → Act → Observe → Think: the standard agent loop across all production agentic systems.
TIER 2
"Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP" — Lewis et al., Facebook AI, 2020
Foundation of enterprise AI architecture // Every RAG system descends from this
Combined parametric (model weights) and non-parametric (retrieved documents) knowledge. The mechanism enabling AI to reason over proprietary institutional knowledge without retraining. For CIB risk: how agents reference current stress scenarios, regulatory guidance, and internal policies rather than relying solely on training data. The institutional knowledge moat.
TIER 2
"Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior" — Park et al., Stanford, 2023
Foundation of persistent agent memory and reflection
Agents with memory streams, reflection, and planning — remembering past actions, reflecting on observations, planning future behaviors. The cognitive architecture behind sophisticated multi-agent systems with long-term institutional memory. Directly applicable to autonomous risk surveillance agents maintaining institutional context over extended time horizons.
TIER 3 — ALIGNMENT & SAFETY
TIER 3
"Mechanistic Interpretability Review" — Bereska et al., 2024 // MIT TR Breakthrough 2026
MIT Technology Review Breakthrough Technology 2026 // Anthropic attribution graphs open-sourced
Comprehensive review of reverse-engineering neural networks into human-understandable algorithms and computations. Defines features, circuits, and the superposition hypothesis. The scientific foundation for Anthropic's goal of "reliably detecting most model problems by 2027." EU AI Act full enforcement August 2026 requires interpretability in high-risk AI — this research is the compliance pathway.
TIER 3
"Concrete Problems in AI Safety" — Amodei et al., OpenAI/Google, 2016
Foundation of AI safety as a research discipline
Defined the five key safety problems: reward hacking, side effects, safe exploration, scalable oversight, and robustness. Still the most cited framing of why capable AI systems might behave unexpectedly despite good training. Every modern alignment research program (Constitutional AI, RLHF, control research) addresses at least one of these five problems.

50B parameters // 363B financial tokens // First dedicated financial frontier model

SCENARIO C — Governance Bottleneck: High-Profile AI Failure Triggers Regulatory Tightening
High-profile AI-related risk failure at a major institution — erroneous stress calculation, LLM-generated model documentation inaccuracy triggering regulatory MRA, or agentic system accessing prohibited data — triggers OCC/Fed response. Prescriptive AI restrictions on capital-adjacent workflows. SR 11-7 LLM addendum imposes significant compliance overhead including mandatory explainability, audit trails, human review requirements adding 18 months to deployment timelines. Trump supply chain designation expands beyond Anthropic. EU AI Act enforcement actions create chilling effect on US institutions with EU operations. Leaders who built AI governance frameworks benefit — they're already compliant. Leaders who deployed without governance face regulatory liability. The cost of retrofitting governance onto already-deployed systems is substantially higher than building it in from the start.
