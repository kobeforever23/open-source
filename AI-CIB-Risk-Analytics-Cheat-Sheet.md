# AI CIB Risk Analytics - Quick Reference Guide

## CORE THESIS
- **Main Idea:** AI should wrap around the risk stack, NOT replace validated quantitative engines
- **Value Proposition:** Move faster, cover more surface area, communicate better while keeping core numbers anchored in controlled models
- **Key Positioning:** "I am not pitching AI as magic. I am arguing that it can materially improve the speed, breadth, and quality of risk work if deployed around the quantitative stack with discipline."

---

## OPENING MOVES (First 2-3 Minutes)

### Frame 1: Market-Anchored
- Reference current market conditions (geopolitical shocks, volatility)
- AI opportunity = compress time-to-scenario
- Generate disciplined, desk-specific shock maps in hours
- **Signals:** You're grounded in today's markets, not generic hype

### Frame 2: Industry Awareness  
- Industry moved from generic copilots → agentic, domain-specific workflows
- Examples: Bloomberg ASKB, Goldman Anthropic agents, JPMorgan production use cases
- Real question: "Where can AI improve decisions WITHOUT creating model risk?"
- **Signals:** You understand vendor landscape and risk-aware deployment

### Frame 3: Governance-First Practitioner
- AI belongs AROUND quantitative stack BEFORE inside it
- Deploy in: scenario generation, explain, triage, governance (first priority)
- Model augmentation comes later, only with controls
- **Signals:** You speak risk-practitioner language, not AI evangelist

---

## WHERE AI ADDS VALUE IN RISK (Deployment Map)

### Tier 1: Immediate Use Cases (High Control, Fast ROI)
- **Scenario Design:** Compress design time, generate shock combinations
- **Risk Explanation:** Translate complex model outputs into decision-ready narratives
- **Limit Triage:** Smart sorting/prioritization of risk exceptions
- **Intelligence Synthesis:** Synthesize market signals, news, data into actionable context

### Tier 2: Governance & Operations
- **Stress Test Automation:** Coordinate multi-desk scenarios
- **Documentation:** Auto-generate audit trails, control matrices
- **Escalation Logic:** Intelligent routing of exceptions to right desk/team

### Tier 3: Advanced (Requires Controls)
- Model augmentation only with robust governance
- Quantitative models stay validated/audited
- AI outputs feed existing decision frameworks

---

## WHAT TO EMPHASIZE

✓ **Speed & Scale:**
- Hours instead of days for scenario generation
- Wider surface area coverage with same headcount
- Real-time desk-specific risk context

✓ **Control & Governance:**
- No black-box replacement of validated models
- Audit trail maintained throughout
- Human-in-the-loop on all material decisions
- Model risk framework applies to AI workflows

✓ **Practitioner Credibility:**
- You understand production constraints
- You know difference between proof-of-concept and production
- You speak to real pain points (speed, coverage, communication)

---

## WHAT TO AVOID

✗ Generic AI hype talk
✗ Promises to replace risk analytics teams  
✗ Black-box LLM recommendations without governance
✗ Overstating AI sophistication in early deployments
✗ Underestimating execution complexity

3. **You've built credible prototypes** inside the exact domain they care about

## ELEVATOR PITCH (30 seconds)

*"We're not replacing risk models with AI. We're deploying AI around the quantitative stack - in scenario generation, risk explanation, limit triage, and governance - so we move faster, cover more ground, and communicate better. The math stays controlled. The workflow gets smarter."*

---

## FOLLOW-UP AMMUNITION

**If they ask about model risk:**
- AI sits outside validated engines
- Outputs feed existing frameworks
- Governance layers maintained
- Audit trail preserved

**If they ask about ROI:**
- Time compression (scenarios: days → hours)
- Coverage expansion (more scenarios, more desks)
- Communication quality (better stakeholder readiness)
- Headcount leverage (same team, wider surface area)

**If they ask about implementation:**
- Start with scenario/explain layers (lowest risk, fast ROI)
- Prove before scaling
- Build controls-first, not features-first
- Measure against existing decision quality

---

## REMEMBER
You're talking to a **senior risk leader**, not a technologist. Lead with **control, speed, and practitioner credibility**. Make it clear you understand this is about smart deployment inside a regulated environment, not chasing technology trends.
