FTQ: The Liquidity Transmission Channel

Flight to Quality is unique in that funding stress IS the scenario. CP market seizure (A1/P1 +200 bp) triggers prime MMF outflows, which forces selling in short-duration corporate bonds. Repo haircuts widen, dealer balance sheets contract, and a liquidity spiral ensues. Credit widens ~124% of Moderate levels despite BETTER macroeconomic fundamentals (GDP still positive at 1.0-1.5%).

Severe Rally: The MBS Convexity Event

When rates collapse 250-320 bp across the curve, mortgage rates approach ~2.5%. The resulting prepayment wave (CPR 40-50) is a defining feature: MBS effective duration collapses from ~5.2Y to 2.5-3.0Y. Portfolios long MBS duration suffer massive negative convexity losses. Meanwhile, OAS widens +70 bp because the convexity cost is repriced, not because of credit risk.

Severe Stagflation: The EM Perfect Storm

Oil at +80% devastates EM oil importers (India, Turkey, South Africa) through twin-deficit channels: fiscal (subsidies) and current account (import bill). Simultaneously, high US rates (FF 4.125%) pull capital from EM, widening EMBI +403 bp to ~753 bp. EM FX -20% creates a negative feedback loop: weaker FX raises import costs, which increases inflation, which constrains EM central banks from cutting, which weighs on growth. Sovereign restructurings likely in frontier markets.

Severe Rally: Oil Collapse Hits Exporters

Oil at -30% creates asymmetric EM impact: exporters (Russia, Nigeria, Middle East) face fiscal crises as revenue collapses, while importers (India, China) receive a partial offset from lower energy costs. EMBI widens +300 bp primarily driven by commodity-exporting sovereigns. DXY weakens only -2% because the USD is pulled between safe-haven demand (supporting) and falling US rates (weakening). Gold surges +25% on both deflation hedge and uncertainty premium.

Negative Swap Spreads: A Signal of Treasury Market Stress

10Y swap spreads are already negative (~-30 bp) reflecting dealer balance sheet constraints and Treasury supply overhang. In severe scenarios, swap spreads widen further negative (-55 to -60 bp) as: (1) Treasury issuance surges for fiscal stimulus, (2) dealers reduce market-making capacity, (3) hedge funds unwind Treasury basis trades, and (4) foreign central banks reduce UST holdings. The CDS-cash basis also widens significantly (+30 to +45 bp in severe scenarios), creating arbitrage opportunities that cannot be exploited due to funding constraints.

Loan vs. Bond: Asymmetric Price Behavior

Leveraged loans exhibit less price downside than comparable-rated bonds in moderate stress (BB loan ~95.5 vs. BB bond ~93.5 in Moderate) due to: (1) floating-rate structure reduces interest rate sensitivity, (2) senior secured status provides structural protection, (3) SOFR floors limit coupon loss. However, in severe stress, loan prices converge toward bond prices as credit fundamentals dominate structure.

Distressed Recovery Assumptions

D/Distressed loans fall to ~25 cents in Severe Stagflation, reflecting: (1) recovery rate compression to 20-25% in rising-rate environment (asset values decline), (2) extended workout timelines (18-24 months), (3) limited DIP financing availability, and (4) operational complexity in stagflationary restructurings where both revenue and margins are impaired.

Why Severe Scenarios Use Flat Scaling

In severe stress (Sev. Rally at 75th pctile, Sev. Stag at 90th pctile), the entire term structure reprices simultaneously. The normal front-end premium breaks down because: (1) forced selling is indiscriminate across tenors, (2) dealer balance sheets are constrained across the board, (3) credit downgrades affect all maturities equally, and (4) liquidity disappears at all points on the curve. The historical analog is March 2020, where IG spreads widened proportionally across 2Y–30Y with correlation approaching 1.0.

Severe Stagflation: Sector Concentration Risk

Three sectors face maximum stress multipliers (1.25x–1.40x) under Severe Stagflation: Energy (commodity whipsaw + CRE exposure), Real Estate (rising cap rates + vacancy + refi wall), and Consumer Cyclical (inflation squeezing margins + consumer credit stress). Combined, these sectors represent ~35% of the IG index by market weight, amplifying portfolio-level impact beyond headline BBB +163 bp.

Defensive Sectors: Natural Hedges

Healthcare (0.70x–0.90x) and Utilities (0.65x–0.85x) consistently receive the lowest multipliers across all scenarios. Their regulated/counter-cyclical demand profiles provide natural portfolio hedges. A BBB Healthcare issuer in Rates Rally would see an effective shock of 0.70 × 33 bp = +23 bp, versus a BBB Energy issuer at 1.05 × 33 bp = +35 bp — a 50% differential at the rating level alone.

Office CRE: The Structural Overhang

Office CMBS faces a unique double stress: (1) Cyclical: economic downturn reduces space demand, and (2) Structural: permanent remote/hybrid work shift has reduced office utilization by ~30% nationally. In Severe Stagflation, office CMBS AAA widens +340 bp — 1.5x the weighted-average AAA of +225 bp — because vacancy rates of 20-25% interact with rising cap rates (from ~6.5% to 8.5-9.0%), severely impairing LTV ratios even for AAA tranches. The 2025 CRE refi wall (~$500B) creates an additional maturity cliff risk.

Starting Spread Adjustment Factor

Current BBB OAS of ~107 bp sits at the ~15th percentile of the 20-year distribution. Historical analogs occurred at wider starting levels (120–180 bp). This means the same percentage move produces a smaller absolute shock today, but the probability of a given widening is higher given how compressed spreads are. Our calibration adjusts for this by: (1) anchoring shocks to the current 107 bp level, (2) verifying target OAS percentiles against the full historical distribution, and (3) ensuring monotonicity (each more severe scenario produces wider target OAS). The GFC peak of 616 bp remains well above our most severe scenario target of 270 bp (Sev. Stag BBB), consistent with our design ceiling below DFAST Severely Adverse.

Calibration Methodology
The 2026 TOH BAU framework uses a multi-anchor calibration approach, combining quantitative percentile targeting with qualitative coherence validation.
Step 1: Historical Distribution
Map 20-year rolling distributions for each risk factor. Identify percentile targets: 65th (Rates Rally) through 90th (Severe Stag).
Step 2: Scenario Anchoring
Anchor to historical analog episodes. Adjust for current starting levels, Fed policy regime, and structural market changes.
Step 3: Cross-Factor Coherence
Verify directional consistency (rates vs. credit vs. funding). Apply correlation structures specific to each macro regime.
Step 4: Monotonicity
Ensure credit shocks monotonically increase across severity tiers per rating. Verify severe/moderate ratios fall within 2.0–2.6x range.
Step 5: Tenor Structure
Apply tenor scaling multipliers to 5Y anchor. Validate front-end premium (1.20x) and back-end discount (0.78x) for non-severe scenarios.
Step 6: Sector Overlay
Apply 10 sector-specific multipliers (0.60x–1.40x) to base rating shocks. Calibrate to sector-level historical beta and fundamental sensitivity.
